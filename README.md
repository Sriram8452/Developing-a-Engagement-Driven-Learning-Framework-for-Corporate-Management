## Existing System

The existing system of corporate training and management learning is predominantly based on traditional, standardized instructional models. These systems rely heavily on uniform workshop-based training formats that follow fixed curriculum and instructor-led delivery. While this approach provides structured knowledge dissemination, it assumes homogeneous learner profiles and does not adequately address differences in experience levels, learning preferences, or role-specific requirements. As a result, learner engagement and practical relevance are often limited.
Another key characteristic of the existing system is the lack of interactive digital interfaces and engagement platforms. Training is commonly delivered through physical classroom sessions or static e-learning modules, offering minimal opportunities for interaction, collaboration, or experiential learning. The absence of digital simulations, adaptive platforms, and real-time engagement tools restricts learners from actively participating in problem-solving or decision-making activities relevant to managerial roles.
The existing system also provides limited hands-on learning and feedback-oriented formats. Training programs focus primarily on theoretical instruction rather than practical application, with few opportunities for learners to engage in realistic business scenarios or simulations. Feedback mechanisms are often delayed or generic, reducing their effectiveness in supporting skill development and behavioral improvement. Consequently, learners may complete training without gaining confidence in applying newly acquired knowledge in real workplace contexts.
In addition, training modules are updated infrequently, making them less responsive to rapid changes in business environments, technology, and organizational strategies. Outdated content, tools, and case studies reduce the relevance and credibility of training programs. This further impacts learner motivation and diminishes the perceived value of corporate learning initiatives.

## Proposed System

For Each Corporate Role:

1. Trainee Gamification Dashboard:
    
It consist of step by step level of evaluation programs.

2. Administrator Dashboard:
    
The administrator can examine the performance of each employee or trainee.

3. Video Content:
   
This contains video lectures, documentations for Employee Corporate Role Training.

4. Cards:
    
The Strategic Thinking & Decision-Making Skills needed for the corporate role is tested from the employee or trainee using Card Interface.

5. Shoot:
   
The Leadership & People Management Skills needed  for the corporate role is tested from the employee or trainee using Shoot Interface.

6. Cricket:
    
The Communication & Stakeholder Management Skills needed for the corporate role is tested from the employee or trainee using Cricket Interface.

7. Forest:
   
The Execution & Operational Management Skills needed for the corporate role is tested from the employee or trainee using Forest Interface.

8. Case:

The Case consist of Case Study Questions in which Interpretation Skills and Strategic Thinking Skills needed for the corporate role is tested from the employee and trainee.
